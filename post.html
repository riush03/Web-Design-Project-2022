Best design i have ever done in my life

By: Denis Muriungi
September 11,2022 -08:25

Even though it burns calories, you’d be forgiven for thinking that a simple stroll might not have a direct impact on shrinking your waistline. Yet research shows that regular walks lead to less body fat, particularly around the middle. One study, published in the Journal of Exercise Nutrition & Biochemistry, found that women with obesity who walked for around an hour three times a week for 12 weeks, on average, saw a significant reduction in both their waist circumference and body fat.

What’s more, with the right technique, you can actually help to strengthen your core while walking. Qualified Pilates instructor Samantha O’Brien (samanthaobrien.live) explains: “To engage the core, brace the abdominal muscles, and to do this imagine you’re squeezing into a tight pair of jeans and zipping up your abdominals at the front, bringing the navel up towards the spine. To make the core work even more, begin to softly swing the arms counter rotating your torso and pelvis to fire up those obliques.”

Want to elevate your walking workouts even further? Try Nordic walking, which is: “A whole-body walking exercise regime based on the summer training of cross-country skiers – the key is to use two specifically designed poles and master the precise technique that ensures you get forward propulsion and not just support from them,” says Gill Stewart, the director of Nordic Walking UK.

“It can also increase the calories burned with every step due to the fact 90 percent of the major muscles are being used, making it good cardiovascular exercise – similar to using a cross trainer.”
